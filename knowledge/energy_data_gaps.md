### ENERGY DATA GAPS

#### 1. **Inaccessible International Energy Forum (IEF) Data**  
**Context:** The IEF is a critical source for global energy policy and coordination, but its data is currently unavailable or inaccessible. This gap hinders the ability to analyze international energy strategies and collaborate across regions.  
**Data Needed:**  
- IEA reports and archived IEF materials (if available)  
- List of key international institutions and their public APIs/annual reports  
- Logs of failed requests (e.g., timeouts, 404 errors) for IEF and other restricted sources  

---

#### 2. **Lack of Regional Energy Poverty Indicators**  
**Context:** Monitoring energy poverty requires granular data on household energy costs, income levels, and regional disparities. Current datasets lack these metrics, limiting the ability to address socio-economic inequalities in energy access.  
**Data Needed:**  
- National and international energy poverty indicators  
- Household energy cost data by region  
- Income distribution statistics and subsidy policies  

---

#### 3. **Incomplete Data on Energy Storage Systems**  
**Context:** The absence of comprehensive data on existing and planned energy storage (e.g., batteries, pumped hydro, thermal systems) limits understanding of grid flexibility and renewable integration.  
**Data Needed:**  
- Installed and planned storage capacity by technology  
- Charge/discharge profiles and regulatory frameworks for storage  
- Regional maps of storage infrastructure  

---

#### 4. **Fragmented Metadata for Energy Data Sources**  
**Context:** The lack of standardized metadata (e.g., field definitions, time resolution, regional coverage) across ENTSO-E, IEA, and NREL datasets complicates data integration and analysis.  
**Data Needed:**  
- Metadata for all fields (e.g., date, region, resource type)  
- Time-series examples from each source (hourly, daily, monthly)  
- Documentation of API formats (CSV, Excel, JSON)  

---

#### 5. **Unstable or Non-Public Data Sources**  
**Context:** Reliance on unstable or non-public sources (e.g., IEF) creates risks of data loss or inaccessibility. A "data risk map" is needed to prioritize alternative sources.  
**Data Needed:**  
- Logs of failed access attempts to critical sources  
- List of high-priority data sources and their current accessibility status  
- Alternatives (e.g., secondary sources, diplomatic channels)  

---

#### 6. **Missing Grid Stability KPIs**  
**Context:** There is no standardized set of metrics (e.g., frequency of outages, reserve capacity, full-load hours) to assess grid stability and resilience.  
**Data Needed:**  
- ENTSO-E and national TSO KPIs (e.g., outage frequency, reserve capacity)  
- Historical outage data and regulatory targets  
- Time-series data on grid performance  

---

#### 7. **Limited Investment Data in Renewables and Infrastructure**  
**Context:** Current datasets lack detailed information on investments in renewables and grid infrastructure, making it difficult to correlate financial flows with grid stress and energy poverty.  
**Data Needed:**  
- IEA and other reports on investment trends  
- National budget allocations for energy projects  
- Data from financial institutions (e.g., World Bank, regional banks)  

---

#### 8. **Absence of Public Energy Dashboards**  
**Context:** The lack of a consolidated, user-friendly dashboard limits the ability to visualize key energy metrics (e.g., renewable penetration, storage capacity) for policymakers and the public.  
**Data Needed:**  
- Consolidated datasets from ENTSO-E, IEA, NREL, and other sources  
- Defined set of KPIs for the dashboard  
- JSON/Markdown templates for reporting  

---

#### 9. **Inconsistent Temporal Resolution Across Sources**  
**Context:** Mismatched time resolutions (e.g., hourly vs. monthly) between ENTSO-E, IEA, and NREL datasets lead to flawed analyses and incorrect trend predictions.  
**Data Needed:**  
- Metadata on temporal resolution (e.g., hourly, daily, monthly)  
- Time-zone alignment information for cross-source analysis  
- Examples of time-series data from each source  

---

#### 10. **Lack of AI/Algorithm Usage Data in Grid Management**  
**Context:** There is no centralized repository of public projects or case studies on AI/optimization algorithms in grid management, limiting the ability to benchmark current capabilities.  
**Data Needed:**  
- Public projects and case studies (e.g., NREL, TSOs, universities)  
- Technical reports on AI applications in grid management  
- Standards for smart grid technologies
